Particles
TLDR
が
が marks the subject — person/thing that performs an action (with verbs) or is what exhibits a certain state (with adjectives/adjectival nouns). By doing so, it is implied that the listener(s) are receiving new information, potentially even the speaker. Contrary to generic statements, it is the objective voice needed in making neutral statements as well as answering questions with the information the asker seeks
ボブ：誰が学生？
Bob: Who is the one that is student?
アリス：ジョンが学生。
Alice: John is the one who is student.
は
は, unlike が, is not a case particle. It is a special kind of particle called a bound particle: its purpose is lived out by the comment that follows, which means it is not restricted by what comes before it. は is bound to the comment that follows. In return, the comment dictates the function of は. The only thing the listener can know is that は marks the topic of the discussion to come. Its motto is 'I don’t know about other things, but as for X...'
ボブ：アリスは学生？
Bob: Is Alice (you) student?
アリス：うん、学生。
Alice: Yeah, (I) am.
も
Another particle that is very similar to the topic particle is the inclusive topic particle. It is essentially the topic particle with the additional meaning of 'also'. Basically, it can introduce another topic in addition to the current topic. Its use is best explained by an example
ボブ：アリスは学生？
Bob: Is Alice (you) student?
アリス：うん、トムも学生。
Alice: Yeah, and Tom is also student.
を
The case that the particle を marks is called the accusative case. The accusative case marks the direct object of a transitive verb. を is usually pronounced as 'o' but is still pronounced as 'wo' depending on the dialect and/or context. Particle を primarily marks the direct object, but that isn’t all it can do. As a transition market it can be used to mean: through/along, toward/around, object of departure - 'from', flux in degree and time
魚さかなfish を 食たべるeat 。
Eat fish.
街まちtown を 歩あるくwalk 。
Walk through town.

Confusing Particle Pairs

は
Subject

Defines topic of a sentence
Defines doer of the action
Emphasises into after は
Emphasises into before が
Clarifes context for what follows
Contains new or important info
Location
で
Location of existence
に
Defines where action takes place
Defines where something is
Used into verbs of action
Used with verbs of existence: あります、います
Time
に
Time
For points in time that occur repeatedly, eg. there are many Sundays, Januarys and 5 o'clocks
For time references that change depending on when 'now' is, eg. 'today', 'next week', and 'last year' are different depending on when they are said
Context tells us which one is being referred to
Destination
に
Direction
へ
Defines a place something is moving to
Defines the direction something is moving to
A destination can also be a direction ( eg. you can move in the direction of a place, such as 'home' ), but a direction is not always a destination ( eg. 'north' is not a place you can go to )
