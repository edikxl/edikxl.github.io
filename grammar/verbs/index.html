Classifying verbs into る and う verbs
If a verb does not end in 「る」, it will always be an う- verb
For verbs ending in 「る」, if the vowel sound preceding the 「る」 is an /あ/, /う/ or /お/ vowel sound, it will always be an う- verb
In other words: ある, うる, おる 🠒 う- verb
いる, える 🠒 る- verb in most cases

Making Nouns Verbs
In English, many words can be used as a noun or verb depending on context, but in Japanese, words usually can't change their part of speech without some sort of change in appearance. For example, the noun for 'yawn' is あくび, but 'to yawn' is あくびをする. Even if 'to do' is not used in any way in English, する is used to help a wide variety of nouns behave as verbs in this manner
Whether you use をする or する is a discussion we'll leave for another time because it can get complicated :c
息いきbreathingをする
To breathe
恋こいloveをする
To be in love
To Cost
When used to mean 'to cost', する can indicate the sticker price of something
このthisワインwineは2ぬ千せん2000円えんyenもevenしませんdoesn't cost
This wine doesn’t even cost 2000 yen.
You could also just use the copula, だ or です, to discuss charges and fees
そのthat料りょお金きんfeeは500ユーロeuroです
That fee is 500 euros.
To Wear (Accessories)
To express 'to wear', you have a lot of options. You can use する for accessories. The other 'to wear' verbs look at To Wear/Take Off
手て袋ぶくるglovesをする
To wear gloves
To be Sensed
The sensing of natural phenomena can be expressed with する. In this usage, the particle を is not used
甘あまいsweet味あじtasteがしますねdoesn't it?
It has a sweet taste, doesn’t it?
To Be... (Occupation/Role)
Whenever you want to say what you do as in what your job/role/occupation is, you typically use する. However, to use this correctly, you need to use the ending ～ている. This is used to show that what you 'do' is an ongoing state
料りょお理り人にんcookをしています
I am a cook
To Be/Have
Another instance in which する may function as 'to be' is in the sense of taking a certain state or condition
冷つめたいcold目めeye(s)をする
To have cold eyes.
来る「くる」
It means 'to come' and is used in much the same way as in English. The only difference is that in its basic understanding as a direction verb, it refers to entities coming toward the speaker. Movement away from the speaker, regardless of the situation, is expressed with 行いくto go
今け朝さmorningサンタさんSantaが来こなかったdidn't come
Santa didn’t come this morning

Modifying nouns with verbs
To modify nouns with verbs you must use the plain form. This works with all verbs of any type
食たべto eatない 肉にくmeat
Meat that I don't/won't eat
These variants are incorrect because should use plain form:
食たべto eatないです 肉にくmeat
食たべto eatません 肉にくmeat
