Basic Questions - か
Just add か to the end and you're done
Noun
高こう校こう生せいhigh school studentですisか
Is a high school student?
Adjective
かわいいcuteですisか
Is cute?
Adjectival Noun
可能possibleかのうですisか
Is possible?
Verb
分わかりますunderstandか
Do you understand?
か does not attach to だ like it does with です. The only time this is acceptable is when か is used to make subordinate clauses. Therefore, だか is wrong and must be changed to either か or dropped entirely. This means it will always attach straight to nouns and adjectival nouns without the copula intervening
か is primarily used in this fashion by male speakers among friends and or toward people of lower status. If this pattern is used toward someone who is not one’s friend nor someone who has a lower status that oneself, the question will create a tone that borders on interrogation, making the speaker sound like a pompous brute, to say the least
君youきみ は あほstupid か
Are you stupid or something?
X は Question Word が Y か
お土産いがげsouvenirs は 何whatなに が いいgood ですis か
Literally: As for souvenirs, what is good?
What would be good for souvenirs?
The particle の is used instead of は below to indicate 'of'
日に本ほんJapan の どこwhere が 好すきlike ですis か
Literally: Where of Japan (you) like?
Or in more natural way: Where is place in Japan (you) like?
What part of Japan do you like?
Question Word + か
Ignoring grammar concerning subordinate clauses you can't simply add か to question words like you can with other nouns in plain speech. Instead, you either need to add the particle よ for exclamatory effect or use だ instead, as non-intuitive as that might seem
何whatなに だis、これthis？
What (the heck) is this?
鍵key(s)かぎ は どこwhere だis(are) よ
Where are the keys!?/Where is the key!?
