Copular Sentences
Therminology
Subject: The person/thing that performs the action or exhibits the description found in the predicate
Predicate: The part of a sentence that gives some information about the subject
Copula: A word used to link the subject and predicate of a sentence
Noun: In its most basic definition, a word that refers to a person, place, thing, event, substance, or quality
Auxiliary: An ending that helps construct verbal conjugations
Definition
This is otherwise known as a 'noun-predicate' sentence. In other words, 'X is Y'. As trivial as it may sound, many far more complex sentences can be broken down to this very structure. The predicate of a sentence may take on different forms depending on what the statement is. When 'Y' is another noun 'copular sentences' can alternatively be called 'noun-predicate sentences'
